## Claim (paper)

[SOURCED] Autoregressive Transformers keep squeezing rich hidden computation through the **token** channel each decode step, so intermediate reasoning state is hard to keep across time.

**T²MLR:** cache a **middle-layer** representation from the **previous token**, fuse it (gated) into an **earlier** layer of the **current** token. Recurrence is temporal (across positions/time), localized to a middle band of layers — not necessarily full-stack looping.

Reported findings (paper abstract / OpenReview TL;DR):

- Beats data- and param-matched Transformer baselines on NL pretrain + multi-hop finetune.
- Looping / recurring **only a middle block** (as little as ~20% of layers) often **beats full-layer recurrence**.
- Can retrofit into a pretrained 1.7B with short finetune.

Core formal idea: indices ℓ_start ≤ ℓ_end, cache R_{t−1} from after ℓ_end at step t−1, fuse into layer ℓ_start at step t via Φ(h, R).

## What this is *not* (for our competition)

Our Easy/Medium setup is **bidirectional over a full prompt**, then score **final answer digits** — not AR CoT decoding. So we cannot drop in `T2MLRWrapper` as-is (causal LM + prev-token middle cache).

Our current UT (`depth_d32_k*_ut`) is **depth recurrence**: same block × K inside one forward, with depth embeddings. Different axis than T²MLR’s **time** recurrence.

## Transferable idea for e1 / e5

Two portable bets (Karpathy: one at a time):

1. **Middle-only depth loop (closest cheap test)**  
   Prefix block(s) once → **shared middle block × K** (+ depth emb) → suffix block once.  
   Paper’s “don’t loop everything; loop the middle” claim, mapped onto depth instead of decode-time.  
   Candidate submission name: `depth_d32_midloop_k4` (vs reference `depth_d32_k4_ut`).

2. **True temporal middle cache along the prompt (heavier)**  
   Left-to-right (or digit-span) recurrent cache: after processing position i’s middle state, fuse into position i+1’s middle — while still allowing bidirectional attn inside blocks if we want. Closer to the paper, more moving parts, easier to waste Easy clock.

**Parent recommendation:** try (1) first on **e1 + e5** only. Hold (2) for Claude Code or a later card.

## Why we are not “near optimal”

Discord meta (`14-discord-beta-meta.md`): Easy/Medium **100%** is often **hardwired solvers**, not learned models. Learned exact-% on Hard tops out tiny after rule patches. Our ~6.5% e1 / ~1% e5 is a **learning** baseline, not a failed race against solver 100%.
